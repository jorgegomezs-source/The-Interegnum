Theory of Transformation & Revolutionary Praxis

The conceptual framework of The Interregnum—where the crisis of existing orders is interrogated not as terminal collapse, but as a specific historical condition demanding new forms of organizational consciousness.

These essays develop the theoretical instruments necessary to grasp this interval "between the collapse of an order and the organization of what comes next," grounding analysis in materialist and situated reflection rather than speculative abstraction. Drawing primarily from Hegelian-Marxist traditions refracted through contemporary conditions, this category examines how self-consciousness becomes organizational capacity—how the "real movement" of existing struggles might anticipate revolutionary practice without falling into either voluntarist fantasy or determinist passivity. Here, theory functions as a diagnostic tool: it maps the uneven terrain where new capacities are forming without yet finding their complete institutional expression, and it interrogates the subjective conditions required for transformation when objective contradictions have matured but subjective forces remain dispersed. These writings serve as the epistemological foundation for the project's broader investigations, insisting that to analyze geopolitical escalation, digital warfare, or imperial decline without a coherent theory of transition is to remain trapped within the very categories we seek to transcend. They ask not just what is collapsing, but what organizational forms are becoming possible within the collapse—and how situated reflection might contribute to their coherence.
